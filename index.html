Converging Technological Trends: Chatbots
Chatbots, or conversational interface agents, rely on the convergence of two massive technological trends in the 21st century: artifical intelligence and messenger apps.

1966
The first chatbot to come close to passing the Turing Test and convincingly imitate a human in conversation using artificial intelligence was ELIZA.
ELIZA was programmed by Joseph Weizenbaum at the MIT Artificial Intelligence Laboratory as a chatbot psychotherapist.
1988

Jabberwacky was first developed by British programmer Rollo Carpenter.
Jabberwacky and Carpenter would go on to compete in artificial intelligence competitions using multiple bot personas, including Cleverbot.
1995
ALICE the Artificial Intelligence Linguistic Computer Entity, is first released by Richard Wallace.
Inspired by ELIZA, and later going on to win the Loebner Prize for artificial intelligence in 2000, 2001, and 2004, ALICE has not passed the Turing Test but is still one of the most accomplished and well-known chat bots.
The Alice conversational archictecture AIML, Artificial Intelligence Markup Language, is used by many modern chatbots.
2001
SmarterChild appears on the friend lists of anyone using AIM or MSN Messenger. Over 50% of US households use AOL and have access to AIM (and SmarterChild)
CONVERGENCE
From this point forward, the development of messenger programs accelerates and increases in popular use while AI development takes a backseat, but both technologies are advancing in parallel.
2008
The App Store is released. There are over 3,000 apps on the store before the end of the year.
Cleverbot is launched.
2010
WhatsApp and Kik launch
First release of Siri
2011
WeChat, Line, Snapchat, Facebook Messenger, and iMessage are all launched
IBM's Watson AI wins Jeopardy! against the best human competitors
2013
A chatbot named Mitsuku wins the Loebner prize for the first time. Mitsuku wins again in 2016 and is runner-up in 2015, and is considered one of the smartest bots online now.
2014
First release of Amazon Alexa
2015
1.5 billion people use messaging apps and 70% of all app use is in less than 200 apps.
Chatbot Rose wins the Loebner prize
2016
6 of the top 10 most used apps are these messaging apps: WhatsApp, Facebook Messenger, Line, Viber, KakaoTalk, and WeChat
Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella says, “Bots are the new apps” and Microsoft's Tay AI is released on Twitter (taken down 16 hours later due to trolls)
2017
With so many people using messenger apps they are the perfect platform for chat bots to grow using human interaction.
Since April, 30,000 bots have been developed for Facebook Messenger alone. Facebook's Wit.ai and Microsoft's Bot Framework development platforms have enabled over 50,000 developers to create chatbots.
